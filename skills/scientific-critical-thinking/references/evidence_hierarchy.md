# Evidence Hierarchy

Higher rank does not automatically mean higher quality, but the hierarchy helps calibrate confidence.

## Common Ordering

1. systematic reviews and meta-analyses
2. randomized controlled trials
3. cohort studies
4. case-control studies
5. cross-sectional studies
6. case series / case reports
7. expert opinion

## What to Check Within Any Level

- was the design executed well?
- is the comparison appropriate?
- are the results consistent with other evidence?
- are the participants, conditions, and measurements relevant to the target claim?
- are the estimates precise?

## When Lower-Level Evidence Can Still Matter

- very large effects
- repeated convergence across different methods
- natural experiments
- settings where higher-level designs are impossible

## GRADE-Style Downgrades

Downgrade confidence for:

- risk of bias
- inconsistency
- indirectness
- imprecision
- publication bias

Upgrade cautiously for:

- very large effects
- dose-response relationships
- multiple converging lines of evidence
