# Common Biases

Use this checklist when a result feels persuasive but fragile.

## Researcher Biases

- **Confirmation bias**: only seeking or highlighting supporting evidence
- **Hindsight bias / HARKing**: rewriting the story after seeing the results
- **Anchoring**: overweighting the first explanation encountered
- **Availability bias**: treating memorable findings as typical
- **Bandwagon effect**: trusting consensus instead of evidence

## Study Biases

- **Selection bias**: the sample differs systematically from the target population
- **Attrition bias**: dropout is unequal or meaningful
- **Observer / detection bias**: expectations affect measurement
- **Performance bias**: groups are treated differently aside from the intervention
- **Measurement bias**: instruments or procedures systematically distort values
- **Reporting bias**: only favorable outcomes or analyses are shown

## Confounding

Ask:

- what variable affects both the explanatory factor and the outcome?
- was it measured?
- was it controlled by design or analysis?
- could it fully explain the effect?

## Mitigation Moves

- preregistration
- blinding
- stronger controls
- explicit alternative hypotheses
- transparent reporting of all outcomes and analytic choices
