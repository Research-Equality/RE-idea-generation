# Experimental Design Checklist

Use this checklist to see whether a study design can actually support its main claim.

## Research Question

- Is it specific?
- Is it answerable with available methods?
- Is it relevant?
- Is it feasible?
- Is it falsifiable?

## Variables

- Are predictor and outcome variables operationally defined?
- Is the primary outcome explicit?
- Are likely confounders identified?
- Are control variables handled by design or analysis?

## Sampling

- Who is the target population?
- Who was actually sampled?
- Is representativeness plausible?
- Was sample size justified?

## Controls and Comparisons

- Is there an appropriate control group or comparison condition?
- Does the design isolate the variable of interest?
- Could reverse causation or selection explain the result?

## Randomization and Blinding

- Was randomization possible and used?
- Was allocation concealed?
- Was blinding feasible and implemented?
- If not, what bias does that introduce?

## Measurement

- Are the instruments valid?
- Are they reliable?
- Are measures direct or just proxies?
- Are outcome assessments standardized?

## Analysis

- Are tests matched to the design?
- Were assumptions checked?
- Are effect sizes and uncertainty reported?
- Was multiple testing handled honestly?

## Final Question

If the answer were the opposite, could this same design still have produced the published story? If yes, the design may be too weak for the claim.
