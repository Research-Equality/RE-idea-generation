# Literature Search Strategies

Ground hypotheses in current evidence before treating them as serious candidates.

## Three-Phase Search

1. **Broad Exploration**
   Understand the landscape, terminology, and major camps.

2. **Focused Search**
   Search for exact mechanisms, pathways, contradictory findings, or analogous systems.

3. **Citation Mining**
   Follow references and cited-by chains from the best papers.

## What to Look For

- recent reviews and meta-analyses
- landmark mechanistic papers
- contradictory findings
- failed explanations
- methods papers that show what can actually be tested

## Query Types

- phenomenon + mechanism
- phenomenon + pathway
- variable A + variable B + association
- intervention + outcome + trial / cohort / review
- anomaly + artifact / bias / confound

## Search Discipline

- begin broad, then narrow
- keep a running list of synonyms
- record exact papers that materially shape the hypothesis space
- explicitly note gaps and contradictions, not just supporting evidence

## Output Habit

For each serious hypothesis project, keep a short evidence log:

```markdown
- Query:
- Key papers:
- What they support:
- What they do NOT explain:
- New terms to search next:
```
